Beetles

External Morphology

Head

Beetle antennae can detect motion, odor, and chemicals. They may also use their anntenae to balance or during mating.

Mouthparts

Beetles have mouthparts similar to grasshopppers. They have two mandibles that grasp, crush, or cut food or enemies. Males generally have bigger mandibles.

Thorax

The thorax is segmented into two.

Prothorax and Pterothorax

The pterothorax is flexibly articulate from the prothorax.

Legs

Beetles have multisegmented legs. They also have claws.

Wings

The forewings of beetles protect their hindwings. The hindwings are what allows for beetles to fly.

Abdomen

The abdomen of the beetle is made up of rings that each have a hole for braething. It is composed of three different segments. The sternum is the most visible part of the abdomen.
