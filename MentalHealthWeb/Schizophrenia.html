Schizophrenia is a disorder involving disorganized thinking and behavior and an abnormal intepretation of reality. Symptoms of Schizophrenia include:


  • delusions (beliefs in things that are not true)
  • hallucinations (hearing or seeing things that are not perceptible to other people, can be with any of the senses)
  • disorganized speech or putting together responses or sentences that are otherwise irrelevant
  • disorganized or unusual behavior
  • loss of interest or withdrawing socially
  • excessive paranoia
  • problems with attention, memory, and organization
  • difficulty expressing emotion
  • reduced ability to function
  • being out-of-touch with reality
  • inability to separate reality from imagination, being unable to tell what is true and false
