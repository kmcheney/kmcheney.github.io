Dissociative Identity Disorder

DID is characterized by switching between identities. For this reason, it was once referred to as Multiple Personality Disorder. Symptoms include:


  • the feeling of having mutiple people living inside your head
  • having various identites with possible differences in voice, gender, name, personal history, features, mannerisms, physical qualities, behavior, memory, etc.
  • bouts of amnesia or having frequent gaps in memory
  • times of confused wandering
  • feeling possessed by another
  • switching between how one presents or what one believes

Dissociative Amnesia

Dissociative Amneia involves memory loss unrelated to medical conditions or general forgetfulness. Symptoms include:


  • episodes in which memory is lost, involving being unable to recall infromation about oneself or events in one's life

Depersonalization/Derealization Disorder

This disorder is characterized by feeling separate from oneself. Symptoms include:


  • feeling one is "outside" of themself
  • feeling like one is seeing their actions, feelings, or self from a distance
  • a feeling of unreality
  • feeling things are foggy or dreamlike
  • time speeding up or slowing down
